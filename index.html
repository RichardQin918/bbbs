About Us

Baby by the Sea offers a different type of spa experience for babies and toddlers. The hydrotherapy service provides a floatation experience for youngsters that is second to none. Before the birth of her son XiaoBao, Cecilia spent 5 years working for a large residential renovation company managing a project coordination team. Zoe has always held an entrepreneurial spirit from running a home-based salon to a flooring company both as a retailer and as a wholesaler and when her daughter Dabao was born Zoe could see the business opportunity available in the baby fitness industry. As mothers, their goal was to create a baby spa with a safe, clean and fun environment for both parents and babies. Benefit

Brain Development in Babies

When youngsters are introduced to the water early they are provided with learning skills that help them transition into their formal schooling years. Cognitive skills that are learned in the water give youngsters an extra edge in terms of problem-solving, working with equations, thinking and learning. This is evident when the children take pre-school testing 5 years later. Children that have been introduced to therapy and massage tend to score better on these pre-entrance tests. Children also develop their coordination and refine their reflexes while controlling their movements in the water.

Massage and Hydrotherapy Benefits for Babies

Hydrotherapy is a term that refers to exercising and floating in water and both neonatal massage and hydrotherapy are developmental techniques that are groundbreaking in nature. A number of different physical, emotional and mental benefits are available to babies and neonates.

Emotional Development

Hydrotherapy for babies helps improve socialization and builds up confidence and trust in others. All senses are stimulated and encouraged through therapy and massage, which in turn makes the baby feel more valued and loved. Water has a natural magic when it comes to improving sleep and improving any discomfort or pain felt from constipation, bloating, cramping or gas. As well, massage and hydrotherapy promote relaxation, which helps with growing pains, teething and muscle spasms. The overall relaxation provided by water begins to regulate emotions in children and improves their memories and attention.

Parental Benefits

As a parent you will experience an intense bonding with your child through communication, observation, relaxation and interaction. You as a parent will experience more self-confidence and improved parenting skills as you socialize with your baby and other parents.
